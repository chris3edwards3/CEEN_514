Importance of Snow Data

Snow Melt is critical to Utah's seasonal stream flow. Sring runoff leads to replenishing of reservoirs, it can also lead to high flows. A recent study has shown that the amount of dust blown into the mountains affects how fast the snow melts. To understand how much water the state will have for the upcoming spring, summer, and fall, it is important to know the quantity of snow.


App Components

The Utah Snow Viewer app will allow users to see how snow pack levels impact watersheds throughout Utah. Hopefully, the user will be able to toggle between snow cover, snow depth, and snow water equivalent (SWE). The average depth and SWE for each watershed will be calculated and shown. The watersheds displayed will be the NRCS Utah Regions.

In addition to the watershed impact, the user will be able to see historical data from SNOTEL stations.
